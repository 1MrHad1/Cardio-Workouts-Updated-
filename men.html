1-PLANK:

How to perform a plank:

  • Take the push-up position and align your butts to your shoulders, to look like a stuff board.
  • Support your body weight with the help of your forearms and toes.
  • Keep your body straight in this position for about one minute and increase the duration by 15 seconds as every passing plank

Benifits of plank :

  1. Builds core muscle strength.
  2. Reduces Back and Spine Pain.
  3. Increases Metabolism.
  4. Gives you a better posture.

2-MOUNTAIN CLIMBERS:

How to perform mountain climbers:

  • Get into an all-fours position with your knees and toes flexed and in contact with the floor. Your hips should be over your knees, and your hands should be slightly wider than your shoulders.
  • Grip the ground with your hand and rotate your shoulders outward to engage your lat muscles.
  • Straighten your legs to lift your knees and enter into a plank position. Your legs should be hip-width apart.
  • Pre-tension your shoulders and hips while engaging your core.
  • Squeeze your quads and glutes while keeping your pelvis slightly tucked, your ribs down, and your chin tucked.
  • While maintaining your alignment, bring one knee towards your chest, then return to the starting position.
  • Repeat the movement on the opposite leg.
  • Alternate your legs at a pace that allows you to maintain alignment, and continue alternating back and forth until you have completed your desired number of repetitions.

3 Benefits of Mountain Climbers:

  1. Incorporating mountain climbers into your workout routine can have several benefits.
  2. Mountain climbers are a full-body exercise. The mountain climber exercise activates muscle groups throughout your entire body, including your triceps, quadriceps, hamstrings, deltoids, and hip flexor muscles.
  3. Mountain climbers can increase your core strength and stability. With proper form, mountain climbers activate core muscles like the transverse abdominis and obliques.
  4. Mountain climbers can improve your cardiovascular health. Mountain climbers can increase your heart rate and calorie burn rate.

3-LEG RAISE:

How to perform Leg Raise:

  • Lie on your back with arms by your sides, palms facing down.
  • Squeeze legs together and flex toes so they’re pointed to the ceiling.
  • Slowly raise your legs until they’re at a right angle with the floor. (If you’re craving more challenge, use your abs to pulse legs and pelvis up toward the ceiling).
  • Slooooowly lower your legs while firmly pressing lower back into the floor.
  • Stop lowering legs once you feel like your back is about to lift off the floor, and then begin lifting legs back up toward the ceiling.

Beneits of Leg Raise:

  1. Helps to lose weight from the legs, and lower abdomen
  2. Tones, strengthens muscles in your abdomen and legs
  3. An effective exercise for lower abs
  4. Can strengthen the hamstrings, calf mucles, and glutes
  5. It is a great exercise that can be done even while lying down in bed
  6. Helps improve focus and attention

Calories Burnt! :

  1. For Every Minute Of Exercise You Brun 8-9 cal.

4-GLUTE BRIDGE:

How to perform Glute Bridge:

  • Lie on your back, with arms by your sides.
  • Bend your knees.
  • Lift off! Press into your feet to slowly lift butt toward the ceiling. Tilt pelvis so you can feel your abs clenching. That’s the sweet spot.
  • Lift hips as high as you can and hold for several seconds.
  • Slowly lower until your back is flat on the floor.
  • Repeat.

Benifits of Glute Bridge :

  1. Reduces lower back Pain
  2. Increases strength and flexibility
  3. Increases strength in abdominal muscle

Calories Burnt! :

  1. For Every Minute Of Exercise You Brun 8 cal.

5-BURPEES

How to perform Burpees:

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower your butt into a squat.
  • As you drop hands to the floor, kick legs behind you so you land in a plank position (power move FTW!).
  • Lower yourself into a push up…
  • …then push back up into a plank.
  • Jump feet up toward hands and stand up.
  • Rinse and repeat.

6-BICYCLE CRUNCHES

How to perform Bicycle Crunches:

  • Lie on your back with knees bent at a 90-degree angle above your hips.
  • Press lower back into the floor and try to keep it anchored there for the entire move.
  • Put hands behind your head and lift shoulders slightly off the floor.
  • While keeping right knee at a 90-degree angle, straighten left leg as you twist left elbow toward right knee. Try to keep right knee directly above your hip rather than pulling it closer to your chest.
  • Repeat on the other side, then continue to alternate sides.
